Domain and Range

The parent function has a vertical asymptote of x=0 and has a horizontal asymptote at y=0. This is because as it’s a reciprocal, as the value of x increases, the value gets smaller and smaller, approaching zero. Therefore, as x is approaching both negative and positive infinity, the number is approaching zero, so, we can just say the horizontal asymptote is y=0. Its domain is {x∈R, x≠0} as the denominator cannot equal zero. If not the whole function would be undefined. The range is {y∈R, y≠0}, for y will never equal zero since no matter how much x increases, the value will forever be approaching zero but never zero. For the domain of the transformation function, I needed to find the points that’s most to the left and most to the right. The point I used most left, was the slight right of the chin of the guy (x=1.75) and the point I used most right was the hairline of the girl (x=3.63). I ended up with a domain of {x∈R, 1.75≤x≤3.63}. For the range, I did the same but used points that were most top and most bottom. The line directly touches the bottom of his ear so I used that as my highest point (4.8), and the line was touching her middle finger, so I used that as my lowest point (1.56). I ended up with a range of {1.56≤y≤4.8}. As mentioned before, the denominator cannot equal zero if not undefined, so we can use that to find our vertical asymptote. I made 1.3(x-2.4)=0, and got x=2.4, making that the vertical asymptote. We can also see it where the graph got moved to the right by 2.4 units, as compared to the parent function where x=0 was the horizontal asymptote, the horizontal asymptote got moved to x=2.4. When the degree of the denominator is bigger than the numerator, y=0 will be its horizontal asymptote as the denominator is growing much faster than the numerator. y will be approaching zero as x is approaching negative/positive infinity.

Trigonometric Function- My lucky flower plush from Popmart

Parent function: f(x)= cot(x)
Transformation function: 12cot(1.4(x-3.43))+2

Transformations

I chose to take a picture with this flower plush because it’s special and somewhat of a lucky charm to me. The plushes were packed in blind boxes and I decided to try my luck. Surprisingly, I ended up getting the exact colour I wanted, which made the plush feel extra meaningful to me. The function I created is f(x) = 12cot(1.4(x − 3.43))+ 2. {1.5 ≤ x ≤ 3.2}, {0.5≤y≤3.06} . The parent function is f(x) = cot(x). Out of all the trigonometry functions, I chose the cotangent function to represent the flower stem because I knew that a cotangent is the reciprocal of a tangent which has an ascending S-shape curve, but in my case I needed a decreasing S-shaped curve. Cotangent is a function that produces an inverted s-shape curve that starts by moving steeply downward, gradually flattens, and moves downwards steeply again. Other trigonometric functions are also unsuitable as a function as the flower stem curve does not have a parabola. Firstly, a vertical compression by a factor of 12 was applied to reduce the steepness of the parent curve to better match the gradual curve of the stem rather than a sharp one. Then, a horizontal compression of k = 1.4 was applied to compress the period so that the cotangent graph fits within the curved section of the stem as the cotangent parent function has a period of π ≈ 3.14 inches, which was too wide to fit within the curvature of the flower stem. After this, to align the curve horizontally so that its shape overlays the flower stem, a phase shift to the right of (x-3.43) was applied. Lastly, to ensure that the curve would be at the right height, which is vertically displaced from the Petronas logo, a vertical translation of +2 was applied to shift the curve upwards.

Domain and Range

The parent function for f(x)= cot(x), has vertical asymptotes at x= nπ (n∈ℤ), which makes it undefined and periodic throughout the entire functions. It also has a domain which is {x ∈ ℝ},{x≠nπ|n∈Z}, meaning it cannot equal to multiples of π because the function is undefined where sin(x)=0. The domain of my function is restricted to {1.5≤x≤3.2}, measured in inches of horizontal displacement from the Petronas logo as origin. For my transformed function, 1.4(x−3.43) is placed between two asymptotes at x= 1.19 and x= 3.43 which ensure only one continuous branch of the function and to avoid the function from extending above my flower stem, approaching the asymptotes or enter a new repeating period, creating a new branch past the asymptote that has with no relation to the flower stem. The range of the parent function's {y ∈ ℝ} as it extends infinitely in both directions without touching the asymptotes. The range of my function is restricted to {0.5≤y≤3.06} measured in inches of vertical displacement above the Petronas logo. As I wasn't able to directly find out the minimum and maximum point directly from desmos, I had to plug in my domain values to get the accurate range for the function. I substituted my domains x values which was {1.5≤x≤3.2} into my transformed function,12cot(1.4(x-3.43))+2. At x=1.5, f(1.5) = 12cot(1.4(1.5−3.43))+ 2≈3.06, and at x=3.2, f(3.2) = 12cot(1.4(3.2−3.43))+2≈0.5. The domain and range restrictions are important to ensure that the function accurately represents the shape of the flower stem’s curve rather than overshooting or having branches that extend beyond the flower stem.

Which in my case it did, as I vertically shifted my graphs upwards by +0.2, the asymptote went from y=0 to y=0.2. However this isn’t really relevant as when I applied my domain, it also indirectly restricted my range making the range for my transformed graph {0.27442≤y≤4.03366}.
